Uncapacitated vs capacitated facilities. Why:
Equilibrium behavior:
Capacitated facilities may lead to more stable equilibria (players avoid mutually destructive overlap), while uncapacitated games might have "all-or-nothing" competition.
Complexity:
Capacitated models require tracking facility loads and redistributing overflow demand, which adds computational overhead.
Realism:
Most real-world facilities (e.g., warehouses, hospitals) have capacity limits. Uncapacitated models are simpler but less practical.
